Success Case: Data Integration and Governance in Multi-Company Environment



Problem Identification

For a corporate group with multiple business lines and various information sources, we addressed the need to structure and standardize their data ecosystem to improve decision-making at a global level.

The company worked with consolidated ERPs such as Navision and Dynamics, integrating information from different operational areas. However, the diversity of systems and structures made budgetary consolidation and cross-business analysis difficult.

Additionally, certain analytical processes, such as calculating raw material and finished product turnover, relied on external tools with multiple manual steps, generating operational complexity and limitations in temporal traceability.

Identified inefficiencies:

  • Heterogeneity in data structures across business lines.
  • Difficulty consolidating information from different systems.
  • Manual analytical processes dependent on external tools.
  • Lack of a common base for global and strategic reporting.

Solution

At CodeGenia, we analyzed the existing technological ecosystem and designed an integration architecture adapted to communications with their ERPs.

We developed an interconnected platform capable of:

  1. Integrating and synchronizing data from different systems.
  2. Standardizing data structures and information families.
  3. Creating a common database oriented towards reporting and analysis.
  4. Automating key analytical processes, eliminating manual tasks.
  5. Improving temporal traceability in strategic calculations such as product turnover.

The solution was implemented without altering already consolidated systems, ensuring operational continuity and minimizing risks.

Results

Thanks to this transformation:

  • The client now has a unified and coherent business vision.
  • Manual workload in data processing was significantly reduced.
  • Information reliability and consistency were improved.
  • A solid foundation was established for future phases of financial consolidation and Business Intelligence.
  • The organization gained the ability to scale and evolve its analytical model without increasing technological complexity.
